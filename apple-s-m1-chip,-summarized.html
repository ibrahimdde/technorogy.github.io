Apple's M1 chip, summarized

04/02/21
Apple's new ARM-based M1 chip is a beast. It comes with an 8-core CPU and an 8-core integrated GPU. It's manifacturing transistor size is 5 nm, and it is pretty powerful. CPU's single-core performance competes with high-end CPU's that we know, and i-GPU is good enough for normal usage or light gaming. It can't be bought seperately, and it comes with MacBook Air's, MacBook Pro's, Mac Mini's, iMac's and iPad's. The power usage of this beast is very low, uses less power than other laptop CPU's or desktop CPU's. It's integral graphics unit's performance is similar to RX 570/GTX 1050 ti of 2016, which is very good considering it is an APU(It should be noted that the current best APU with good integral graphics unit is AMD Ryzen 4750g, which performs worse than M1 chip despite being a full-sized chip) that uses fracture of the power of those cards while processing both computing works and graphical works.

Some iPad pro's will come with this chip, which is very big news, considering tablets were considered bigger sized phones. This will help corporates that uses the tablet very often for their jobs, or graphical designers who uses the tablet for drawing.
